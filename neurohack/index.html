Boston Precision Brain & Mental Health Hack 2025

Advanced Neuroscience Workshop

Boston Precision Brain & Mental Health Hack is a three-day event happening at Northeastern University this Fall designed as an extension to Brainhack Global. This event will bring together students, postdocs, and faculty across fields and universities in Boston to tackle precision medicine problems through hackathon-style projects, hands-on tutorials, and discussions. Following the Brainhack spirit, this hackathon is not a “coding sprint” or exclusive to programmers—neuroscientists, psychologists, engineers, programmers, students, faculty, and more can and do make a significant contribution to the event. Given some methodological overlap and goals, we are excited to incorporate both digital health and neuroscience approaches.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Who should attend the Boston Precision Brain & Mental Health Hack 2025?

This workshop is designed for undergraduate students, graduate students, postdoctoral researchers, faculty members, and industry professionals working in neuroscience or related fields. Whether you're just starting your research journey or are an established scientist, you'll find valuable opportunities to learn cutting-edge techniques, share your work, and build meaningful connections within Boston's vibrant neuroscience community.

Will you provide lunch?

Yes. We will provide coffee for everyone and lunch for those who filled out the inscription form before the deadline

What's the workshop format?

The Boston Precision Brain & Mental Health Hack features a dynamic mix of keynote presentations by leading researchers, hands-on technical workshops, interactive discussion panels, and dedicated networking sessions. You'll have opportunities to present your own work, collaborate on new projects, and engage in both formal presentations and informal conversations with peers and mentors from across Boston's research institutions.
